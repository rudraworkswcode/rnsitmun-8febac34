### RESOLUTION FORMAT GUIDE

A resolution is a formal document that contains all the actions a committee wants to take regarding a topic. It's divided into three main parts:

1. **Heading** - Basic information about the resolution
2. **Preamble** - Background information and reasons for action
3. **Operative** - Specific actions to be taken

## SUB-CLAUSES

Sub-clauses provide details about the main operative clause. They are marked with lowercase letters.

1. *Requests* the Secretary-General to:
   a) Create a task force to monitor implementation;
   b) Submit annual reports to the General Assembly;
   c) Allocate necessary resources for this initiative;

2. *Calls upon* all Member States to:
   a) Take necessary legislative measures;
   b) Cooperate with international organizations;
   c) Provide financial and technical support;

## EXAMPLE RESOLUTION

**Committee:** United Nations Human Rights Council

**Topic:** Combating Human Trafficking

**Sponsors:** United States, United Kingdom, Germany

**Signatories:** France, Japan, Brazil, Australia, Canada, India, South Africa, Mexico

---

### THE HUMAN RIGHTS COUNCIL,

*Deeply concerned* by the increasing rates of human trafficking affecting millions worldwide,

*Recognizing* that human trafficking is a violation of fundamental human rights and dignity,

*Recalling* United Nations General Assembly Resolution 64/293 on the UN Global Plan of Action to Combat Trafficking in Persons,

*Noting with regret* that current efforts have been insufficient to address the root causes,

*Believing* that international cooperation is essential to combat this transnational crime,

*Aware of* the particular vulnerability of women and children to trafficking,

1. *Calls upon* all Member States to strengthen their national legislation against human trafficking;

2. *Establishes* an International Task Force on Human Trafficking to:
   a) Coordinate global anti-trafficking efforts,
   b) Share best practices among nations,
   c) Monitor implementation of anti-trafficking measures;

3. *Urges* Member States to provide adequate resources for victim rehabilitation and support;

4. *Requests* the Secretary-General to submit an annual report on the progress of anti-trafficking efforts;

5. *Encourages* collaboration between governments, civil society, and international organizations;

6. *Recommends* that Member States implement public awareness campaigns about human trafficking;

7. *Decides* to remain actively seized on this matter.

## FORMATTING RULES

✅ **DO:**
- Use formal, diplomatic language
- Be specific in your operative clauses
- Number operative clauses
- Use semicolons (;) after each operative clause except the last
- End the last operative clause with a period (.)
- Use preambulatory phrases in italics
- Underline operative verbs

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use first person ("I", "we", "our country")
- Use contractions ("don't", "can't")
- Use informal language
- Number preamble clauses
- Forget punctuation

---

## TIPS FOR WRITING STRONG RESOLUTIONS

1. **Research thoroughly** - Know what's been done before on this topic
2. **Be specific** - Vague clauses are weak clauses
3. **Be realistic** - Your solutions should be feasible
4. **Work with others** - Coalitions make stronger resolutions
5. **Address funding** - Explain how your solutions will be financed
6. **Follow format** - Judges notice proper formatting
7. **Proofread** - Grammar and spelling matter
