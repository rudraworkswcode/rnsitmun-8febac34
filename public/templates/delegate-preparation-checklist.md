## üìö PHASE 1: RESEARCH (4-6 weeks before conference)

### Understanding Your Committee
- [ ] Read the background guide thoroughly (at least 3 times)
- [ ] Understand the committee type (GA, SC, ECOSOC, etc.)
- [ ] Learn the Rules of Procedure for your committee
- [ ] Research the committee's mandate and powers
- [ ] Identify the committee's past actions on similar topics

### Country Research
- [ ] Study basic country profile (government, economy, population)
- [ ] Research country's foreign policy priorities
- [ ] Find your country's official position on the topic
- [ ] Review past UN voting record on related issues
- [ ] Identify your country's allies and adversaries
- [ ] Research regional bloc membership (EU, AU, ASEAN, etc.)
- [ ] Find recent statements from country's leaders
- [ ] Study your country's relationship with other major powers

### Topic Research
- [ ] Understand the history of the issue
- [ ] Identify root causes of the problem
- [ ] Research current situation and recent developments
- [ ] Find key statistics and data
- [ ] Study past UN resolutions on this topic
- [ ] Research what other organizations have done (WHO, World Bank, etc.)
- [ ] Identify main stakeholders and affected populations
- [ ] Understand different perspectives on solutions

### Documentation
- [ ] Compile credible sources (UN, government, academic)
- [ ] Take organized notes with proper citations
- [ ] Create timeline of major events
- [ ] List key facts and statistics
- [ ] Save important quotes from leaders/experts
- [ ] Bookmark useful websites

---

## ‚úçÔ∏è PHASE 2: WRITING (2-3 weeks before conference)

### Position Paper
- [ ] Outline your position paper
- [ ] Write first draft
- [ ] Include country's official position
- [ ] Cite sources properly
- [ ] Propose 2-3 realistic solutions
- [ ] Proofread for grammar and spelling
- [ ] Get feedback from advisor/teacher
- [ ] Revise and finalize
- [ ] Submit before deadline
- [ ] Print extra copies for conference

### Speech Preparation
- [ ] Write opening statement (30 seconds - 1 minute)
- [ ] Prepare key talking points
- [ ] Write sample speeches for different scenarios
- [ ] Prepare closing/summary statement
- [ ] Practice speeches out loud
- [ ] Time your speeches
- [ ] Record yourself and review
- [ ] Memorize key points (don't read verbatim)

---

## üé≠ PHASE 3: SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (Ongoing)

### Public Speaking
- [ ] Practice speaking clearly and slowly
- [ ] Work on voice projection
- [ ] Practice making eye contact
- [ ] Reduce filler words ("um", "like", "you know")
- [ ] Practice confident body language
- [ ] Learn to handle nervousness
- [ ] Join speaking club or practice with friends

### Negotiation & Diplomacy
- [ ] Learn to build consensus
- [ ] Practice active listening
- [ ] Study negotiation tactics
- [ ] Learn to compromise without abandoning core positions
- [ ] Practice forming alliances
- [ ] Learn to read body language
- [ ] Understand power dynamics in negotiations

### Parliamentary Procedure
- [ ] Memorize basic motions
- [ ] Understand voting procedures
- [ ] Know how to make points and yields
- [ ] Practice raising points of order/personal privilege
- [ ] Understand precedence of motions
- [ ] Learn proper diplomatic language
- [ ] Review hand signals and gestures

---

## ü§ù PHASE 4: STRATEGY (1-2 weeks before)

### Alliance Building
- [ ] Identify potential allies
- [ ] Research positions of other countries
- [ ] Plan initial contacts and talking points
- [ ] Identify countries to avoid/oppose
- [ ] Prepare coalition-building strategy
- [ ] Create list of countries to approach first

### Resolution Strategy
- [ ] Draft preliminary resolution ideas
- [ ] Identify clauses you want included
- [ ] Prepare amendments for other resolutions
- [ ] Practice resolution writing format
- [ ] Identify "red lines" (non-negotiables)
- [ ] List areas where you can compromise

### Contingency Planning
- [ ] Prepare for unexpected questions
- [ ] Develop responses to counterarguments
- [ ] Plan for crisis scenarios (if in crisis committee)
- [ ] Prepare backup solutions
- [ ] Think about "what if" scenarios
